Educational Theories & Curriculum Mechanisms

An interactive, offline single-page application to explore the core pillars of psychological and pedagogical theory.

1. Jean Piaget: Cognitive Development

Piaget's theory suggests that children move through four different stages of cognitive development. Explore the stages below:

Sensorimotor stage (0-2): Infants learn about the world through their senses and actions (grasping, looking). Object permanence develops.
2. Alison Gopnik: The Scientist in the Crib

Gopnik argues that young children learn about the world in much the same way that scientists do—by conducting experiments, analyzing statistical data, and forming intuitive theories.

3. Kurt Lewin: Leadership Styles

Lewin identified three primary styles of leadership: Autocratic, Democratic, and Laissez-faire. Adjust the slider to see how the classroom climate changes.

Autocratic Democratic Laissez-Faire
Democratic: High student engagement, moderate to high productivity, high morale, and independent thinking.
4. Carl Rogers: Humanistic Reflection

Rogers emphasized unconditional positive regard, empathy, and congruence. Use this journal to reflect on your practice. (Your data autosaves locally).

5. Deci & Ryan: Self-Determination Theory

Intrinsic motivation is driven by three basic psychological needs: Competence, Autonomy, and Relatedness.

6. Arendt & Biesta: Educational Purpose

Hannah Arendt spoke on the crisis in education—balancing the protection of the child and the world. Gert Biesta outlines three domains of purpose: Qualification, Socialization, and Subjectification.

Teacher Cheat-sheet

Print this page (Ctrl+P). Interactive elements will be hidden automatically.

Theory / Concept Core Objective Application Tip
Piaget Match tasks to cognitive stages. Use concrete props/visuals for concrete operational phase.
Gopnik Encourage intuition and hypothesis testing. Create "sandboxes" for safe failure and experimentation.
Lewin Balance structure and freedom. Utilize democratic leadership for long-term engagement.
Rogers Foster a humanistic environment. Practice active listening and non-judgmental feedback.
Deci & Ryan Promote internal motivation (SDT). Offer choices (Autonomy) and build relationships (Relatedness).
Arendt & Biesta Determine the 'why' of the lesson. Don't focus solely on qualification; allow subjectification.
Curriculum Align what is planned with what is learned. Be mindful of the 'hidden curriculum' (implicit norms/values).
